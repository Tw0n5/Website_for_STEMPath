01 — What Is STEMPath

A GPS for your career journey.

Most Caribbean STEM students and early-career professionals are unsure which skills they are missing, which courses to take next, and how to compete in a skills-first job market. STEMPath solves that problem in minutes!

Think of it like Google Maps, but for career development. You tell it where you are (your skills, courses, GPA) and where you want to go (Software Engineer, Data Scientist, Biomedical Engineer…). STEMPath then calculates the fastest, cheapest, and most achievable route to get you there.

No more sifting through generic job advice. No more guessing if your degree is enough. STEMPath uses your career and course information to recommend personalized step-by-step pathways that help you reach your STEM career goals.

Built for the Caribbean context, the platform accounts for regional and US labor markets, financial constraints and varying educational backgrounds. Therefore, making STEMPath a career guidance platform specially designed for you.

02 — How It Works

From profile to pathway in 4 steps.

No complicated setup. Just tell STEMPath about yourself and let the algorithm do the heavy lifting.

01

Create Your Profile

Register and enter your degree, completed courses, certifications, and current skills. Or simply upload your resume the system extracts your skills automatically.

02

Choose Your Target Career

Browse 900+ STEM occupations from Cybersecurity Analyst to Biomedical Engineer. Filter by category, search by keyword, and select the role you're aiming for.

03

Take the Proficiency Quiz

Answer 6 targeted questions about your chosen career's foundational skills. Your score determines your starting node in the learning graph beginner or intermediate.

04

Get Your Optimal Pathway

Dijkstra's algorithm computes the shortest, cheapest, or most balanced route from where you are to where you want to be. Visualize, save, export, and track your progress.

# Weighted graph: skills as nodes, courses as edges def compute_pathway(user_skills, target_career, mode): graph = build_graph(skills, courses) # Assign edge weights by optimization mode if mode == 'time': weights = [c.duration for c in courses] elif mode == 'cost': weights = [c.price for c in courses] else: # balanced weights = multi_criteria(time, cost, difficulty) source = map_user_node(user_skills) target = map_career_node(target_career) # Dijkstra: O((V + E) log V) return dijkstra(graph, source, target, weights)
Under the Hood

The Science Behind the Suggestion

STEMPath isn't guessing. It models your career journey as a weighted directed graph. This is a mathematical structure used in mapping, logistics, and network routing . It also applies Dijkstra's shortest-path algorithm to find your optimal route.

Skills and certifications are nodes. Courses are edges. Time, cost, and difficulty are weights. You choose how to optimize, and the algorithm guarantees the mathematically best path.
